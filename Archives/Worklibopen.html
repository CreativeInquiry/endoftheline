Workshop Sites: Carnegie Libraries

Beechview Library Participants

 In the summer of 1996, Lisa Link and Carolyn P. Speranza conducted six free art workshops at the following Carnegie Branch Libraries: Allegheny, Beechview, Hazelwood, Homewood, Lawrenceville, and West End. The artists publicized the workshops with flyers and announcements in each neighborhood. At the workshops, participants were introduced to the art of collage. The artists also interviewed the participants on tape cassettes about important issues in their neighborhoods. From these discussions of past and present neighborhood issues and photographs, several themes emerged that were common in all neighborhoods: neighborhood gardening, community leaders, urban removal and community-based urban renovation. The resulting five collages that recirculated back to neighborhoods on buses in May of 1997, were direct results of visual and audio materials contributed by participants at each workshop.
